# Discovery Brief

Client slug: `aqualoop-hospitality`
Date: 2026-07-03
Owner: Discovery Lead Agent

## Client Snapshot

AquaLoop Hospitality is an early-stage product idea for capturing and treating greywater from hotels or buildings for approved non-potable reuse. The project is pre-architecture, pre-segment validation, and pre-commercial proof.

## Engagement Objective

Create an evidence-graded discovery package and deck draft that helps decide whether hotels are the right first wedge, which product architecture deserves validation, what proof is missing, and which claims can safely be made.

## Central Discovery Question

How should AquaLoop Hospitality position, package, and present onsite greywater reuse so that hospitality owners and operators believe it can reduce water exposure and strengthen sustainability performance despite regulatory complexity, installation friction, and unproven product economics?

## Situation Summary

The client hypothesis is directionally plausible but not proven. Hotels are worth investigating because they have multiple water-use areas, ongoing operating exposure, sustainability programs, and property engineering teams (`CLIENT-001`, `SRC-001`, `SRC-011`, `SRC-012`). However, the product's launch segment, architecture, treatment technology, code pathway, capex/opex model, buyer, and proof standards are all unresolved.

## Working Hypotheses

| Hypothesis | Evidence Needed | Decision It Affects | Current Confidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hotels are the best first wedge compared with residential buildings. | Hotel water baseline, buyer interviews, retrofit feasibility, budget owner, utility economics. | Segment priority and deck narrative. | Low to medium |
| A centralized or semi-centralized system is more credible than per-room modular units for hotel adoption. | Engineering feasibility study, installation workflow, plumbing constraints, maintenance model, capex comparison. | Product architecture. | Low |
| Sustainability and operating-risk narrative should lead before pure savings claims. | Buyer interviews, certification/reporting needs, proof of water reduction potential. | Positioning and messaging. | Medium |
| Regulatory pathway is a market-entry gate, not a generic feature. | Launch jurisdiction, AHJ guidance, plumbing/public-health review. | Go-to-market sequencing and claims. | Medium |
| The AquaLoop name requires review. | Trademark/legal search and competitor naming audit. | Brand and deck naming. | Low |

## Evidence Plan

Market research should investigate:

- Hotel water-use areas and conservation framing (`SRC-001`, `SRC-002`).
- Sustainability certification pressure and water criteria (`SRC-011`, `SRC-012`).
- Jurisdiction-specific onsite reuse rules (`SRC-013`, `SRC-014`).

Competitor benchmarking should investigate:

- Named onsite water reuse vendors and their proof language (`SRC-006` to `SRC-010`).
- Existing AQUALOOP naming in the greywater market (`SRC-015`).
- Status quo water conservation alternatives.

Offering architecture should investigate:

- Centralized, semi-centralized, per-room, and hybrid system options.
- Retrofit versus new-build priority.
- Monitoring, maintenance, certification, and installation responsibilities.

Client clarification needed:

- Launch geography.
- First hotel segment.
- Product architecture appetite.
- Target economics.
- Technical partner or engineering assumptions.
- Brand/legal constraints.

## Decision Criteria

The final strategy should be judged by:

- Evidence of a specific buyer with a clear operational or sustainability job.
- Plausible code and approval path in a selected jurisdiction.
- Credible product architecture and installation model.
- Proof plan that can support savings, impact, safety, and maintenance claims.
- Competitive differentiation that does not depend on unsupported superiority claims.

## Risks

| Risk | Potential Impact | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Regulatory assumptions are generalized across jurisdictions. | Misleading feasibility claims and client-ready blocker. | Pick one launch market and review local rules with experts. |
| Savings narrative is asserted without property data. | Investor/client trust risk. | Build property-specific model using water bills, fixture counts, occupancy, tariff, and capex/opex. |
| Architecture choice is made before engineering validation. | Product rework and installation risk. | Run concept engineering and installation pathway study. |
| Naming conflicts with existing AQUALOOP systems. | Brand/legal risk. | Conduct legal search before naming deck is externalized. |
