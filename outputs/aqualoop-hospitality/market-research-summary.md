# Market Research Summary

Client slug: `aqualoop-hospitality`
Date: 2026-07-03
Researcher: Market Researcher Agent

## Research Scope

Discovery question: Should AquaLoop lead with hotels/hospitality as the first wedge for onsite greywater reuse, and what evidence is needed before making client/investor claims?

Included markets/audiences: Hotels, hospitality groups, commercial buildings, onsite non-potable water reuse, greywater reuse, sustainability certification context.

Excluded areas: Full market sizing, jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction code review, engineering feasibility, financial modeling, customer interviews, and legal review.

## Executive Findings

1. Hotels are a credible discovery wedge because water is operationally relevant across guest rooms, restrooms, laundry, kitchens, landscaping, and mechanical systems, but this does not prove AquaLoop's economics (`SRC-001`, `SRC-002`).
2. The strongest near-term narrative is not "guaranteed savings"; it is "reduce water exposure through a validated onsite reuse pathway" with proof still to be developed (`CLIENT-001`, `SRC-003`, `SRC-005`).
3. Regulatory and water-quality requirements are launch-market dependent, making geography selection a first-order strategy decision (`SRC-013`, `SRC-014`).
4. Existing vendors already claim onsite reuse, greywater recycling, and shower recycling territory, so AquaLoop needs sharper segment focus, proof, and architecture rationale (`SRC-006`, `SRC-008`, `SRC-009`, `SRC-010`).
5. The AquaLoop name carries possible conflict risk because AQUALOOP appears in market use for greywater reuse technology (`SRC-015`).

## Handoff To Strategy

Most important implication: Position hotels as the first validation wedge, not as a proven market decision. The deck should sell the disciplined validation path.

Claims that require verification:

- Any savings percentage or gallons saved.
- ROI or payback.
- Regulatory compliance.
- NSF/ANSI certification or equivalence.
- Installation simplicity.
- Hotel buyer willingness to pay.
- Competitive superiority.
