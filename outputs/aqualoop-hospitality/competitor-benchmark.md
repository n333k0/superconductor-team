## Summary

Table stakes:

- Water reuse or conservation benefit.
- Non-potable use framing.
- Sustainability language.
- Building integration.
- Proof, monitoring, and reliability expectations.

Overused category moves:

- Broad sustainability claims.
- Generic water savings language.
- Smart/circular water language without buyer-specific proof.
- Vendor-led performance claims without independent context.

Credible whitespace:

- Hotel-first validation and architecture logic.
- Clear "what we can prove now / what must be validated next" proof ladder.
- Regulatory-first launch-market narrative.
- Buyer-specific story for owners, operators, engineering teams, and sustainability leaders.

## Positioning Implications

AquaLoop should not lead with generic "greywater recycling system" language. The stronger territory is a hotel-specific water resilience and reuse-validation platform: a product concept designed around hospitality operating realities, regulatory gating, and measurable proof.

## Offer Implications

The first offer should be a validation package before a product sale:

- Property water opportunity assessment.
- Architecture-fit study.
- Jurisdiction/code pathway review.
- Financial model and proof roadmap.
- Pilot specification.

## Visual Direction Implications

Avoid generic blue-water eco imagery. Use building-section diagrams, hotel operating zones, proof ladders, and risk-gate visuals.

## Watchouts

Claims that are weak, dated, or need verification:

- Any quantified savings.
- Any installation simplicity.
- Any universal regulatory approval.
- Any superiority over named competitors.
- Any brand/name availability.

## Benchmark Completeness

- Named direct competitors reviewed: 5 vendor/source sets.
- Indirect substitutes reviewed: Low-flow fixtures, conservation programs, status quo.
- Status quo options reviewed: Yes, at category level.
- Evidence gaps that block client-ready claims: Independent competitor validation, buyer interviews, price/capex comparison, property economics, legal review.
