## Summary

Table stakes:

- Job status visibility
- Scheduling or dispatch coordination
- Mobile team enablement
- Workflow configuration
- Reporting

Overused category moves:

- Broad all-in-one claims
- Screenshots without outcome framing
- Feature-led navigation
- Generic productivity language

Credible whitespace:

- Frame the category around preventing coordination breakdowns.
- Show how implementation support accelerates operational adoption.
- Connect field execution to customer promise delivery.

## Benchmark Matrix

## Positioning Implications

Northstar should avoid competing on feature breadth alone. A stronger position is "field execution confidence for organizations where missed updates become customer and executive problems."

## Offer Implications

The offer should distinguish between the platform, launch configuration, and ongoing operating support. This makes implementation feel intentional rather than vague.

## Visual Direction Implications

Avoid generic dashboard montages as the primary visual language. Use maps, handoff diagrams, escalation paths, and operating rhythm visuals that show coordination across teams.

## Watchouts

Claims that are weak, dated, or need verification:

- Any claim that Northstar is more enterprise-ready than established suites.
- Any claim that implementation is faster than competitors.
- Any claim that customer escalations are reduced without customer evidence.
