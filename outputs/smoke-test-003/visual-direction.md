# Visual Direction

Client slug: `smoke-test-003`
Date: 2026-07-03
Visual director: Visual Director

## Strategic Role Of Visuals

The deck should make Northstar feel like an operating visibility system for serious distributed teams. Visuals should clarify coordination across field, customer, and executive layers rather than decorate generic SaaS claims.

## Category Visual Audit Summary

Common visual conventions:

- Dashboard screenshots in laptop frames
- Blue-heavy enterprise SaaS palettes
- Abstract workflow icons
- Generic productivity illustrations
- Dense feature grids

Conventions to avoid:

- Leading with product UI before the strategic problem is clear
- Overusing "real-time visibility" dashboard imagery
- Treating field teams as generic mobile workers without operational context

Useful conventions to keep:

- Clear process diagrams
- Operational status indicators
- Structured tables for proof and decisions
- Simple maps or region-based visuals when discussing distributed teams

## Direction Principles

1. Show coordination paths, not isolated features.
2. Make operating risk visible before introducing the platform.
3. Use proof and decision visuals that feel executive, not product-marketing heavy.

## Color Behavior

Use a restrained neutral base with high-contrast signal colors for operational states: clear, at risk, blocked, resolved. Avoid a monochrome blue SaaS look.

## Typography Direction

Use a practical, legible sans-serif system with strong hierarchy. Titles should read as executive assertions. Tables and matrices should prioritize scanning over decorative styling.

## Imagery Direction

Favor real operational context when available: field teams, service locations, dispatch environments, customer communication moments. If real imagery is unavailable, use abstracted operational diagrams rather than stock-style team photography.

## Diagram And Data Visualization

- Handoff-to-escalation flow
- Status quo risk balance
- Positioning box
- Offer ladder
- Proof roadmap
- Decision checklist

## Slide Composition

Use one dominant idea per slide. Keep evidence tables compact. Use left-to-right flows for operational sequences and vertical ladders for offer architecture.

## Accessibility And Production Constraints

- Maintain strong contrast between text and background.
- Do not rely on color alone to communicate status.
- Keep body text large enough for conference-room review.
- Mark unverified metrics as needed inputs, not claims.
