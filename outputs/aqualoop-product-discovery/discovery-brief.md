# Discovery Brief

Client slug: `aqualoop-product-discovery`
Date: 2026-07-03
Owner: Discovery Lead Agent

## Client Snapshot

AquaLoop Hospitality is an early-stage industrial product concept for onsite greywater capture and non-potable reuse in hotels, hospitality properties, and potentially other multi-unit buildings. The idea is pre-architecture, pre-engineering, pre-regulatory validation, pre-buyer validation, and pre-commercial proof.

## Engagement Objective

Create a complete, evidence-gated discovery package that helps the client decide whether hospitality is the right first market, what product architecture options deserve validation, which claims are safe, what proof is missing, and how to frame the opportunity in a premium agency strategy deck.

## Central Discovery Question

How should AquaLoop position, package, and present onsite greywater reuse so that hospitality owners, operators, and technical stakeholders believe it is worth validating as water-resilience infrastructure despite regulatory complexity, installation disruption, maintenance uncertainty, and unproven economics?

## Situation Summary

The product problem is real enough to investigate because onsite non-potable reuse is a recognized category and hotels have recurring water-use contexts (`SRC-001` to `SRC-007`). But the actual AquaLoop opportunity is not proven. Segment fit, buyer ownership, local code pathway, technical architecture, maintenance model, manufacturing model, pilot economics, and brand name remain unresolved (`CLIENT-001`, `ASSUMPTION-001` to `ASSUMPTION-005`).

## Evidence Plan

Market research should investigate:

- Hotel water-use and conservation context (`SRC-001`, `SRC-002`).
- Onsite non-potable reuse category definitions (`SRC-003`, `SRC-004`, `SRC-007`).
- Sustainability program language for hospitality (`SRC-013` to `SRC-015`).
- Segment comparison across hotels, multifamily, resorts, student housing, wellness, commercial buildings, and public facilities.

Competitor benchmarking should investigate:

- Named onsite reuse and greywater vendors (`SRC-016` to `SRC-023`).
- Low-friction substitutes such as fixtures, water audits, leak detection, and conventional conservation (`SRC-001`, `SRC-002`, `SRC-024`).
- Status quo procurement logic: municipal potable water/sewer plus incremental conservation.

Product architecture should investigate:

- Source-to-use loops.
- Centralized, semi-centralized, per-room, retrofit, new-build, and managed-service options.
- Installation access, controls, monitoring, service intervals, and failure modes.

Client clarification needed:

- Launch geography.
- First property type and pilot partner profile.
- Target source water and reuse applications.
- Engineering partners or assumptions.
- Desired business model and risk appetite.
- Legal/brand stance on the AquaLoop name.

## Decision Criteria

The final strategy should be judged by:

- Specific buyer and influencer clarity.
- Plausible source/demand match.
- Jurisdiction-specific code path.
- Installation and maintenance feasibility.
- Evidence-backed competitive differentiation.
- Ability to produce client-safe claims without unsupported metrics.
- MVP design that validates the riskiest assumptions first.

## Risks

| Risk | Potential Impact | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hospitality is assumed instead of tested. | Wrong first market and wasted pilot spend. | Compare segments using buyer access, economics, source/demand fit, and installability. |
| Code pathway is generalized. | Misleading feasibility and external-claim risk. | Select one launch jurisdiction and validate with AHJ/regulatory specialists. |
| Architecture is selected before engineering. | Rework, retrofit infeasibility, service burden. | Run concept engineering across architecture options. |
| Savings or ROI are implied without property data. | Trust, legal, and investor credibility risk. | Build model only after collecting water bills, tariffs, occupancy, fixture counts, capex, and opex. |
| Vendor landscape is treated as independently verified. | Benchmark overclaims. | Caveat vendor self-positioning and add independent evidence before client-ready delivery. |
| AquaLoop name conflicts with existing market use. | Brand/legal risk. | Conduct legal/trademark review before external naming. |
