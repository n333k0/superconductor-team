# Visual Direction

Client slug: `aqualoop-product-discovery`
Date: 2026-07-03
Visual director: Visual Director Agent

## Strategic Role Of Visuals

The web preview should make an undefined industrial product feel tangible without pretending the product is proven. The design should behave like a premium strategy/product design agency deck: confident, structured, high-contrast, and explicit about proof gates.

## Category Visual Audit Summary

Common visual conventions:

- Blue water drops, ripples, leaves, circular arrows, green-building icons.
- Technical product renders and plumbing diagrams.
- Sustainability certification badges and generic eco photography.

Conventions to avoid:

- Decorative water imagery that implies proof.
- Fake metrics or fake dashboard screenshots.
- Generic SaaS dashboard cards.
- Stock luxury-hotel photography that distracts from operations.
- Overstuffed tables with tiny caveats.

Useful conventions to keep:

- Building-section diagrams.
- Source-to-use loops.
- Product architecture matrices.
- Proof gates and readiness badges.
- Competitive territory maps.
- Pale blue insight cards for opportunity or proof-needed moments.

## Direction Principles

1. Make the building the product context.
2. Make uncertainty visible and useful.
3. Use evidence badges as a core visual system.
4. Separate opportunity, proof, and blocked claims.
5. Feel like industrial strategy, not green marketing.

## Color Behavior

Use the ticket-directed design token system:

- `--bg: #f4f5f2`
- `--ink: #070807`
- `--panel-dark: #0b0d0c`
- `--panel-soft: #f8f8f4`
- `--line: rgba(7,8,7,0.14)`
- `--muted: #5e6460`
- `--blue: #d7eef8`
- `--blue-strong: #b9d8e8`
- `--card-radius: 28px`
- `--pill-radius: 999px`
- `--shadow-soft: 0 24px 80px rgba(0,0,0,0.12)`

Use black cards for decisive thesis and risk moments, off-white cards for research content, and pale blue panels for opportunity, metrics placeholders, and insight callouts.

## Typography Direction

- Oversized editorial headlines.
- Tight but readable line height.
- Letter spacing remains `0`.
- Compact uppercase metadata labels.
- Slide numbers as large translucent numerals.
- Strong hierarchy for title, key message, content cards, and evidence footer.

## Imagery Direction

Avoid stock-photo dependency. Use schematic diagrams and structured cards first. If images are added later, prioritize real property infrastructure, hotel mechanical areas, MEP-style drawings, or client-approved product/installation visuals.

## Diagram And Data Visualization

- Hotel source-to-use loop.
- Segment comparison matrix.
- Buyer decision chain.
- Architecture option matrix.
- Regulatory gate sequence.
- Competitor territory map.
- MVP proof roadmap.
- Ready/blocked readiness ledger.

## Slide Composition

- Full-width deck canvas with large slide cards.
- Alternating dark and light slide treatments.
- Two-column and three-column grids.
- Metric/insight cards even when the metric is a placeholder marked "proof needed."
- Footer area for evidence refs and readiness labels.
- Blocked slides should use deliberate risk styling and "Validation required" labels.

## Accessibility And Production Constraints

- High contrast between text and panels.
- No tiny unreadable evidence text.
- Do not rely on color alone for blocked or ready states.
- Use responsive grids so content does not overlap on mobile.
- Keep deck content concise enough for preview cards.
