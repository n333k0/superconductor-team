# Buyer And User Strategy

Client slug: `aqualoop-product-discovery`
Date: 2026-07-03
Owner: Market Researcher + Strategy Synthesizer

## Buyer Diagnosis

The buyer is not yet validated. AquaLoop should assume a multi-stakeholder hotel decision chain until interviews prove a narrower owner. The core buying tension is that water reuse may appeal to ownership, operations, sustainability, and facilities for different reasons, while each group can block the project for disruption, cost, compliance, or maintenance risk (`CLIENT-001`, `ASSUMPTION-005`).

## Likely Decision Chain

| Role | Likely Job | Decision Power | Core Objection | Proof Needed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Owner / asset manager | Improve property economics, resilience, value, and ESG story. | Budget approval or sponsor. | Payback, capex, risk, asset disruption. | Property financial model and risk register. |
| Corporate operations | Standardize property performance and avoid operational failures. | Can sponsor or block. | Complexity, reliability, brand risk. | Pilot plan and operating protocol. |
| General manager | Protect guest experience and property operations. | Can block property participation. | Noise, downtime, complaints, staff burden. | Install plan and failure-mode plan. |
| Director of engineering / facilities | Maintain systems safely and reliably. | Technical gatekeeper. | Maintenance, access, alarms, code, service burden. | Engineering review and service model. |
| Sustainability / ESG lead | Support water stewardship and reporting. | Narrative sponsor. | Greenwashing or unsupported impact claims. | Measured baseline and approved claims. |
| MEP / plumbing consultant | Design or review system fit. | Feasibility gatekeeper. | Constructability and code pathway. | Concept drawings and jurisdiction assumptions. |
| AHJ / code official | Protect public health and code compliance. | Approval gate. | Treatment, cross-connection, monitoring, operations. | Jurisdiction-specific submission pathway. |

## First Buyer Hypothesis

Primary initial sponsor: hospitality owner/operator or asset-management group with a property where water exposure, sustainability pressure, or development/renovation timing makes reuse worth investigating.

Primary technical veto: director of engineering/facilities plus MEP/plumbing reviewer.

Status: needs validation through interviews.

## Segment Priority Recommendation

| Priority | Segment | Why | Caveat |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Full-service hotels / resorts in water-sensitive jurisdictions | Strongest combined hypothesis for use cases, operations, and sustainability narrative. | Needs property economics and code review. |
| 2 | New-build hospitality or multifamily | Easier integration path may exist before construction. | Longer sales cycle and specification influence needed. |
| 3 | Student housing | Concentrated use and institutional sustainability may fit. | Procurement and seasonality require validation. |
| 4 | Gyms/spas/wellness | Shower-heavy use case is intuitive. | Smaller economics and hygiene perception risk. |
| 5 | Broad commercial/public facilities | Category relevant but less focused. | Too broad for first MVP. |

## Buying Friction Map

| Friction | Buyer Reaction | Required Countermove |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "Will this disrupt guests?" | Property teams resist pilot. | Installation/disruption plan and pilot scope. |
| "Who maintains it?" | Facilities resists ownership. | Managed service option and maintenance schedule. |
| "Is this legal here?" | Owners and MEP pause. | Jurisdiction-first review. |
| "Does it pay back?" | Asset manager blocks capex. | Property-specific financial model; no generic ROI claim. |
| "Can we claim sustainability value?" | ESG lead hesitates. | Approved claim hierarchy and measured proof. |
| "Why not install low-flow fixtures?" | Procurement defaults to cheaper alternatives. | Show where baseline conservation ends and reuse begins. |

## Interview Plan

Minimum interviews before client-ready strategy:

- 3 hotel owner/asset-management stakeholders.
- 3 property operations or general manager stakeholders.
- 3 engineering/facilities stakeholders.
- 2 sustainability/ESG stakeholders.
- 2 MEP/plumbing/code specialists.
- 2 non-hotel adjacent buyers: multifamily developer/operator and student housing or resort operator.

## Interview Evidence Goals

- Budget owner and approval path.
- Trigger events: renovation, new-build, water restriction, ESG target, high water/sewer cost.
- Accepted pilot scope and disruption limits.
- Maintenance tolerance and service preferences.
- Alternatives considered first.
- Required proof for capex, pilot, and claims.

## Positioning Guidance

Lead with:

- "Validate a property-specific water reuse pathway before making savings claims."
- "Reduce uncertainty around code, architecture, and operations."
- "Make water-reuse decisions evidence-led."

Avoid:

- "Guaranteed savings."
- "Plug-and-play hotel retrofit."
- "Compliant everywhere."
- "Sustainability impact proven."
- "Buyer-ready product."

## Handoff

Buyer strategy is `internal-draft-ready`. It is not client-ready because the buyer chain, budget owner, willingness to pay, and pilot approval path are unvalidated.
