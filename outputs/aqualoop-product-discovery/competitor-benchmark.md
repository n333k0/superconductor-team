## Summary

Table stakes:

- Clear source/end-use framing.
- Non-potable reuse language.
- Regulatory and treatment credibility.
- Building integration story.
- Monitoring, operations, or maintenance plan.
- Proof that goes beyond sustainability aesthetics.

Overused category moves:

- Generic blue-water imagery.
- Broad "save water" and "sustainable building" claims.
- Smart/circular-water language without buyer-specific proof.
- Retrofit simplicity claims unsupported by property context.
- Vendor performance claims without independent verification.

Credible whitespace:

- Hotel-first proof pathway.
- Regulatory-first market entry.
- Architecture choice justified by property type.
- Explicit "safe to say / blocked until proven" claim discipline.
- Buyer map that respects owner, operator, engineering, sustainability, MEP, and code roles.

## Positioning Implications

AquaLoop should not lead with "greywater recycling system" as the whole story. The more credible position is a hospitality water-reuse validation system: a code-aware, property-specific pathway from water baseline to architecture decision to pilot proof.

## Offer Implications

The first offer should be packaged as:

1. Water Reuse Opportunity Assessment.
2. Jurisdiction and Architecture Fit Study.
3. Pilot Specification and Proof Plan.
4. Optional managed pilot / system partner model after validation.

## Visual Direction Implications

Avoid generic eco water visuals. Use hotel section diagrams, source-to-use loops, architecture matrices, proof gates, competitor territory maps, and readiness badges.

## Watchouts

Claims that are weak, dated, or need verification:

- Quantified water savings or payback.
- Installation cost or simplicity.
- Certification/compliance.
- Maintenance interval or service burden.
- Performance superiority over named competitors.
- Legal availability of the AquaLoop name.

## Benchmark Completeness

- Named direct/adjacent competitors reviewed: Epic Cleantec, Hydraloop, Greyter, Orbital Systems, INTEWA AQUALOOP, ReWater Systems, Nexus eWater.
