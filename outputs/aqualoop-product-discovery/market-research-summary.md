# Market Research Summary

Client slug: `aqualoop-product-discovery`
Date: 2026-07-03
Researcher: Market Researcher Agent

## Research Scope

Discovery question: Is hospitality the right first wedge for AquaLoop, and what must be validated before the product can be positioned externally?

Included markets/audiences: Hotels, resorts, multifamily, student housing, gyms/spas/wellness properties, commercial buildings, public facilities, onsite non-potable water reuse, greywater reuse, water conservation, hospitality sustainability, and building infrastructure buyers.

Excluded areas: Full market sizing, legal opinion, jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction code review, engineering design, financial model, lab/testing proof, customer interviews, and manufacturing estimate.

## Executive Findings

1. Hospitality is a credible first validation wedge because hotels have repeated water-use contexts and sustainability language, but this does not prove payback, installability, or buyer willingness (`SRC-001`, `SRC-002`, `SRC-013`, `SRC-014`, `CLIENT-001`).
2. Onsite non-potable reuse is a recognized category with public-agency, association, and standards context; AquaLoop should describe a pathway to compliance and certification rather than claim compliance (`SRC-003` to `SRC-007`).
3. Launch geography is a first-order strategy choice because local programs and state/public-health rules can materially shape allowed sources, end uses, treatment, monitoring, operations, and permits (`SRC-008` to `SRC-012`).
4. Named competitors and adjacent analogues already occupy generic "greywater," "water recycling," and "onsite reuse" language, so AquaLoop needs hospitality-specific proof and architecture logic rather than generic sustainability claims (`SRC-016` to `SRC-023`).
5. The strongest near-term package is an assessment-to-pilot pathway: source/demand baseline, jurisdiction review, architecture fit, buyer validation, and measured proof roadmap (`ASSUMPTION-001` to `ASSUMPTION-005`).

## Handoff To Strategy

Most important implication: AquaLoop should be positioned as a rigorous validation pathway for hospitality water reuse, not as a ready-to-sell product.

Claims that require verification:

- Any savings percentage, gallons saved, ROI, payback, tariff impact, or incentive eligibility.
- Any certification, compliance, or water-quality claim.
- Any installation simplicity or retrofit compatibility claim.
- Any maintenance interval or service-cost claim.
- Any buyer willingness-to-pay claim.
- Any competitive superiority claim.
