## Engagement Objective

Northstar Fieldworks needs a sharper market position, clearer offer architecture, and an executive-ready strategy deck before launching a new enterprise sales push.

## Business Context

Confirmed facts:

- The company helps organizations coordinate field teams, job status, customer updates, and operational reporting.
- The current story over-indexes on software features and under-explains operational outcomes.
- Sales conversations are strongest when prospects have visible field coordination pain.
- Leadership wants to move from "workflow tool" language toward a more strategic operations platform narrative.

Client-stated goals:

- Clarify who the company is best for.
- Explain the combined software and implementation model without making the offer feel heavy.
- Create a deck that sales and leadership can use in enterprise conversations.

Known constraints:

- Avoid claiming category leadership without proof.
- Avoid a full rebrand recommendation in this phase.
- Preserve enough continuity for the existing sales team to adopt the story.

## Audience Context

Primary audience:

Operations executives at companies with distributed field teams and complex customer commitments.

Secondary audiences:

- Regional operations directors
- Customer experience leaders
- IT stakeholders involved in tool approval

Buyer pains, jobs, or triggers:

- Poor visibility into job status
- Manual handoffs between field, dispatch, and customer teams
- Customer escalations caused by missing updates
- Difficulty proving operational performance across regions

## Current Offer Context

Current products/services:

- Field operations platform
- Implementation onboarding
- Workflow configuration
- Customer success support

Pricing or packaging signals:

Pricing is not included in the sample. Current packaging appears to be demo-led rather than organized into clearly named engagement layers.

Sales/channel context:

Enterprise selling relies on founder, marketing, and customer success involvement.

## Competitors And Alternatives

Direct competitors:

- Field service management platforms
- Vertical-specific operations tools

Indirect alternatives:

- CRM workflow modules
- Project management tools
- Internal spreadsheets and dispatch boards

Status quo option:

Continue coordinating work through existing tools, manual updates, and regional workarounds.
