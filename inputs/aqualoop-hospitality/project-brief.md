# Project Brief

Client slug: `aqualoop-hospitality`
Date: 2026-07-03
Prepared by: Discovery OS

## Project Name

AquaLoop Hospitality

## Working Idea

Develop an onsite greywater capture, treatment, and reuse system for hotels, buildings, or hospitality groups. The system would redirect treated non-blackwater sources, such as showers and sinks, toward non-potable operational uses such as toilet flushing, cleaning, irrigation, or other approved use cases.

## Discovery Goal

Create a strategy/deck-style discovery package that converts the raw product idea into:

- A segment and opportunity hypothesis.
- A buyer and decision-maker map.
- A competitor/substitute benchmark.
- A technical and operational architecture exploration.
- A regulatory and proof-risk map.
- A business model and positioning recommendation.
- A deck outline and `slides.json` that can render in the local web preview.

## Decision To Unlock

Should AquaLoop initially pursue hotels/hospitality as the first wedge for onsite greywater reuse, and what evidence is needed before turning that wedge into a client-ready or investor-ready strategy?

## Working Segment Hypotheses

| Hypothesis | Current Evidence | Confidence | Status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hotels are a stronger first wedge than broad residential buildings. | Client intuition plus public category evidence that hotels have multiple major water-use areas and sustainability programs. | Low to medium | Needs validation |
| Centralized or semi-centralized systems may be more operationally credible than per-room systems for hotels. | Architecture reasoning only; no engineering design, capex, or installation data provided. | Low | Needs engineering validation |
| Buyer value should combine operating exposure, sustainability reporting, and visible guest/property differentiation. | Client brief plus public sustainability/certification context. | Low to medium | Needs buyer interviews |
| Regulatory pathway is a launch-market decision, not a generic claim. | Public regulatory examples show jurisdiction-specific rules. | Medium | Needs launch-market choice |
