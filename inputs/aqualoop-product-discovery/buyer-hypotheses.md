# Buyer Hypotheses

Client slug: `aqualoop-product-discovery`
Date: 2026-07-03

## Primary Buyer Hypothesis

The primary economic buyer for a hotel-first AquaLoop pilot is likely a hotel ownership, asset-management, or corporate operations stakeholder who cares about operating cost exposure, asset value, sustainability commitments, and capital planning.

Status: `ASSUMPTION-005`; needs buyer interviews.

## Influencer Hypotheses

| Role | Likely Concern | Likely Influence | Evidence Status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| General manager / property operator | Disruption, guest experience, downtime, maintenance burden | Can block pilots that create operational risk | assumption |
| Director of engineering / facilities | Plumbing access, treatment reliability, service routines, alarms, safety | Critical technical gatekeeper | assumption |
| Sustainability / ESG lead | Water stewardship, reporting, certifications, brand narrative | Can sponsor narrative and internal support | assumption supported by sustainability-program context |
| Asset manager / owner | Capex, payback, property value, risk | Likely budget authority for retrofits | assumption |
| MEP engineer / plumbing consultant | System design, code pathway, constructability | Required for feasibility | assumption |
| Code official / AHJ | Permitting, cross-connection, water quality, monitoring | Approval gate | external-sourced category risk |
| Developer / design team | New-build specification and lifecycle cost | More relevant for new construction | assumption |

## Segment Buyer Hypotheses

| Segment | Potential Buyer | Why It Might Fit | Why It Might Fail | Current Readiness |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Full-service hotels | Owner/operator, asset manager, engineering leader | Multiple water-use zones and maintenance teams | Retrofit complexity and capex approval | internal-draft hypothesis |
| Boutique hotels | Owner/operator | Brand sustainability story may matter | Smaller scale may weaken economics | needs-validation |
| Resorts | Owner/operator, sustainability lead | Irrigation/laundry/pool/spa demand may exist | Site-specific complexity and seasonal occupancy | needs-validation |
| Multifamily | Developer/owner/operator | Repeated units and new-build planning | Residential code, tenant disruption, buyer fragmentation | needs-validation |
| Student housing | Owner/operator, university/public-private operator | Concentrated use and sustainability goals | Budget/procurement complexity | needs-validation |
| Gyms/spas/wellness | Owner/operator | Showers create visible reuse opportunity | Smaller total demand and brand hygiene concerns | needs-validation |
| Commercial buildings | Building owner/facilities | Existing onsite reuse category | Less guest-facing story; lower source/demand match may vary | needs-validation |
| Municipal/public facilities | Public works/facilities | Public sustainability and resilience goals | Procurement slow; political and compliance burden | needs-validation |

## Interview Targets

- Hotel owner or asset manager.
- Hotel director of engineering.
- Hotel sustainability/ESG lead.
- MEP engineer with hotel retrofit experience.
- Plumbing/code consultant.
- Multifamily developer or asset manager.
- Resort operations leader.
- Water reuse system installer/operator.

## Interview Questions

- What water costs or restrictions are visible enough to drive action?
- Which non-potable uses are acceptable operationally?
- What disruption would be unacceptable?
- Who owns the budget and who can veto the project?
- What proof would be required before a pilot?
- What procurement path would a pilot follow?
- Which alternatives would be considered first?
- What maintenance burden is tolerable?
- How would a sustainability story be used, if at all?
