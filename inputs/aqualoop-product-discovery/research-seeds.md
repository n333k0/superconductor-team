# Research Seeds

Client slug: `aqualoop-product-discovery`
Date: 2026-07-03

## Research Priorities

1. Market context for onsite water reuse, greywater reuse, non-potable reuse, and hospitality sustainability.
2. Segment comparison: hospitality, multifamily, resorts, student housing, wellness/gym properties, commercial buildings, and public facilities.
3. Buyer decision chain for hotels and property infrastructure.
4. Direct and adjacent competitors in greywater and onsite reuse.
5. Indirect substitutes and status quo alternatives.
6. Regulatory/code uncertainty and jurisdiction dependency.
7. Installation, retrofit, maintenance, and operations risk.
8. Product architecture and MVP validation options.
9. Manufacturing and service-model implications.
10. Proof roadmap for safe claims.

## Seed Source Categories

- Public agency and conservation sources: EPA WaterSense, EPA onsite non-potable reuse, local water agencies.
- Standards and certification: NSF/ANSI 350, IAPMO/ASSE onsite non-potable water reuse personnel credentialing, ICC/IAPMO code context where available.
- Industry associations: WateReuse Association, Alliance for Water Efficiency, hotel sustainability organizations.
- Hospitality sustainability programs: Green Key Global, GSTC, LEED water efficiency context.
- Competitor/vendor pages: Epic Cleantec, Hydraloop, Greyter, Orbital Systems, INTEWA AQUALOOP, ReWater, Nexus eWater, onsite reuse integrators.
- Substitutes: low-flow fixtures, audits, leak detection, smart metering, rainwater harvesting, municipal reclaimed water, baseline conservation.

## Search Questions

- What public sources describe water use and conservation opportunities in hotels?
- How is onsite non-potable water reuse defined and governed?
- What standards or certification pathways are relevant to onsite residential/commercial water reuse systems?
- Which cities or states have specific onsite water reuse requirements or examples?
- Which vendors already occupy greywater, onsite reuse, or shower recycling territory?
- How do vendors describe proof, installation, monitoring, and maintenance?
- What are common barriers to greywater reuse adoption in buildings?
- Which property segments have the strongest source/demand match for non-potable reuse?
- What evidence would a hotel owner/operator need before approving a pilot?

## Evidence Discipline

- Use vendor pages only to document vendor positioning and category language.
- Use public agency, standard, code, or regulator sources for regulatory and category context.
- Treat local rules as examples, not universal rules.
- Mark market sizing and savings claims as blocked unless source-specific and relevant.
