# Product Requirements

Client slug: `aqualoop-product-discovery`
Date: 2026-07-03
Status: Internal discovery requirements; not an engineering specification.

## Product Mission

Explore whether AquaLoop can become a hotel-first greywater reuse product or service that captures approved non-blackwater sources, treats or manages them as required, and redirects water to approved non-potable uses in a way that is technically feasible, code-aligned, operationally maintainable, and economically defensible.

## Required Jobs To Validate

| Job | Requirement | Status |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Source capture | Identify which hotel greywater sources can be captured without unacceptable plumbing disruption. | needs-validation |
| Demand matching | Match reusable water supply to approved non-potable demand such as toilet flushing, irrigation, or cleaning. | needs-validation |
| Treatment pathway | Define treatment, storage, monitoring, and safety requirements for selected source/end-use/geography. | blocked until jurisdiction selected |
| Installation pathway | Compare retrofit, renovation, and new-build installation complexity. | needs-validation |
| Maintenance model | Define who maintains filters, treatment equipment, sensors, storage, alarms, and service intervals. | needs-validation |
| Buyer proof | Build evidence that target buyers value the system enough to approve pilot/capex/service spend. | needs-validation |
| Claim safety | Create measured proof before savings, ROI, ESG, or compliance claims are externalized. | blocked until pilot/model |

## Functional Requirement Hypotheses

- Capture greywater from defined non-blackwater sources.
- Separate treated/reused water from potable systems with required controls.
- Route output only to approved non-potable uses.
- Include monitoring or inspection features if required by code or service model.
- Support maintenance access without disrupting guest experience.
- Provide property-level reporting only after data collection is validated.

## Non-Functional Requirement Hypotheses

- Must be reliable enough for hotel operations.
- Must avoid odor, noise, downtime, cross-connection, guest-experience, and brand-risk issues.
- Must fit property maintenance workflows.
- Must provide clear fail-safe behavior.
- Must be financeable or piloted without overclaiming ROI.
- Must be supportable by install/service partners.

## MVP Requirement

The first MVP should not be a fully commercialized system. It should validate:

1. A specific property water baseline.
2. A specific source-to-use loop.
3. One launch jurisdiction pathway.
4. One architecture option against installation and maintenance constraints.
5. Buyer willingness to pilot.
6. A proof model for future savings and impact claims.

## Explicit Non-Requirements For This Package

- Final engineering design.
- Final treatment technology selection.
- Final compliance opinion.
- Certification claim.
- Manufacturing plan.
- Unit economics.
- Installation cost.
- Savings percentage.
- Customer testimonial.
