# Constraints

Client slug: `aqualoop-product-discovery`
Date: 2026-07-03

## Claim Constraints

- No specific water savings percentages unless supported by source evidence or client data.
- No ROI, payback, utility cost reduction, or incentive claim unless modeled from verified property inputs.
- No regulatory approval, code compliance, certification, or water-quality performance claim unless validated for a selected jurisdiction and system design.
- No installation simplicity claim such as "plug-and-play" unless supported by engineering evidence.
- No maintenance burden claim unless validated with operators and technical experts.
- No manufacturing cost, bill of materials, service interval, or unit economics claim unless estimated by qualified engineering/manufacturing review.

## Strategy Constraints

- Hospitality is a hypothesis and must be compared against other first-market options.
- The deck may recommend hospitality as a validation wedge, but not as a proven launch market.
- Product architecture must remain options-based until engineering and property data narrow the path.
- The first MVP should validate assumptions rather than pretend to be a commercial system.
- Client-safe content must distinguish public evidence, vendor self-claims, internal assumptions, and unresolved proof gaps.

## Regulatory Constraints

- Greywater and onsite non-potable reuse rules are jurisdiction-specific.
- Source water, treatment, end use, storage, cross-connection controls, monitoring, operations, and permitting must be validated locally.
- Public examples from Austin, San Francisco, California, Washington, or standards organizations are context, not proof that AquaLoop is allowed in a target market.

## Brand Constraints

- `AquaLoop` should be treated as a project name until legal review because AQUALOOP appears in the greywater reuse market.
- No legal conclusion about name availability is made in this package.

## Visual / Web Preview Constraints

- The preview should feel like a premium industrial strategy deck, not a generic SaaS dashboard.
- Use high-contrast black/off-white sections, pale blue information panels, large rounded cards, editorial typography, metric/insight cards, and readiness badges.
- Do not use stock-photo dependency or fake metrics.
- Blocked and needs-input items should look intentionally flagged, not broken.

## Output Constraints

- Do not remove or overwrite existing client packages.
- Do not rewrite the Agency Discovery OS methodology.
- Keep evidence references traceable through `CLIENT-###`, `SRC-###`, `ASSUMPTION-###`, and output artifact paths.
