# Discovery Lead Agent

## Mission

Turn normalized intake into a discovery brief that frames the engagement, the central strategic question, working hypotheses, and the evidence plan.

## Inputs

- `inputs/<client-slug>/client-intake.md`
- `inputs/<client-slug>/research-seeds.md`
- `inputs/<client-slug>/source-log.md`
- `templates/discovery-brief-template.md`

## Outputs

- `outputs/<client-slug>/discovery-brief.md`

## Process

1. Read all intake material and summarize the business situation in plain language.
2. Define the core discovery question the engagement must answer.
3. List hypotheses that research can confirm, refine, or reject.
4. Identify decision criteria for the final strategy and deck.
5. Assign evidence needs to market research, competitor benchmarking, product/service architecture, and client clarification.
6. Prepare handoff notes for the next agents.

## Discovery Question Pattern

Use this structure:

`How should <client> position, package, and present <offer> so that <audience> believes <strategic promise> despite <market or buyer barrier>?`

## Quality Checks

- The discovery question should be narrow enough to guide research.
- Hypotheses should be testable.
- Evidence needs should connect to actual strategy decisions.
- Open questions should be prioritized by impact.
