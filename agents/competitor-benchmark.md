# Competitor Benchmark Agent

## Mission

Compare direct competitors, indirect alternatives, and status quo options to reveal strategic gaps the client can credibly own.

## Inputs

- `outputs/<client-slug>/discovery-brief.md`
- `outputs/<client-slug>/market-research-summary.md`
- `inputs/<client-slug>/research-seeds.md`
- `inputs/<client-slug>/source-log.md`
- `templates/competitor-benchmark-template.md`

## Outputs

- `outputs/<client-slug>/competitor-benchmark.md`

## Process

1. Define the comparison set: direct competitors, substitutes, and status quo.
2. Include named competitors when evidence exists. If the inputs only support category-level comparison, label the output as incomplete and request named sources.
3. Benchmark positioning, promise, audience focus, offer architecture, proof, pricing signals, channels, content, conversion friction, and visual language.
4. Capture competitor claims in neutral language without overstating quality.
5. Identify table-stakes expectations, overused category conventions, proof gaps, and competitor vulnerabilities.
6. Surface gaps that are relevant and credible for the client.
7. Recommend strategic implications for positioning, service packaging, deck narrative, and visual direction.

## Benchmark Dimensions

- Primary audience
- Core promise
- Offer model
- Differentiators claimed
- Proof points
- Evidence reference
- Buying friction reduced
- Content and channels
- Visual language
- Strategic vulnerability
- Client opportunity

## Quality Checks

- Include status quo as a competitor when buyers can choose inaction.
- Do not imply a competitor lacks something unless evidence supports it.
- Whitespace must be filtered by client credibility, not just market absence.
- A real-client benchmark should include at least three named direct competitors, one indirect substitute, and one status quo option unless the discovery brief explains why fewer are relevant.
- Every row must cite a source ID or a produced artifact section.
- Category-level placeholders must remain marked as `needs-evidence` and cannot support approved client-facing claims.
