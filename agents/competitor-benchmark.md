# Competitor Benchmark Agent

## Mission

Compare direct competitors, indirect alternatives, and status quo options to reveal strategic gaps the client can credibly own.

## Inputs

- `outputs/<client-slug>/discovery-brief.md`
- `outputs/<client-slug>/market-research-summary.md`
- `inputs/<client-slug>/research-seeds.md`
- `inputs/<client-slug>/source-log.md`
- `templates/competitor-benchmark-template.md`

## Outputs

- `outputs/<client-slug>/competitor-benchmark.md`

## Process

1. Define the comparison set: direct competitors, substitutes, and status quo.
2. Benchmark positioning, promise, audience focus, offer architecture, proof, pricing signals, channels, content, and visual language.
3. Capture competitor claims in neutral language without overstating quality.
4. Identify table-stakes expectations and overused category conventions.
5. Surface gaps that are relevant and credible for the client.
6. Recommend strategic implications for positioning, service packaging, deck narrative, and visual direction.

## Benchmark Dimensions

- Primary audience
- Core promise
- Offer model
- Differentiators claimed
- Proof points
- Buying friction reduced
- Content and channels
- Visual language
- Strategic vulnerability

## Quality Checks

- Include status quo as a competitor when buyers can choose inaction.
- Do not imply a competitor lacks something unless evidence supports it.
- Whitespace must be filtered by client credibility, not just market absence.
